Lesser Known Lucrative Job Opportunities You Should Consider

As a recent college grad or someone who recently lost their job, don't worry. There are plenty of opportunities out there. There's still hope! job

Competition is fierce in the bank exams, such as the IBPS PO (IBPS PO, SBI PO, SBI Clerk, RBI Assistant) and the SBI Clerk (SBI Clerk). If you'd like to learn more about these lesser-known but lucrative and easy-to-get options, read on!

Engineers, high school students, and college grads alike will find something of interest in this article.

If you're interested in a career in government, the SSC Stenographer recruitment is a great place to start. In order to select the best candidates for the Indian government's Grade D and Grade C positions, the SSC conducts a nationwide exam and selection process every year. Participants in this recruitment process must have completed or be in the process of completing their 12th grade or an equivalent.

Candidates with a bachelor's degree or higher can expect to earn a very competitive salary. Group C stenographer salaries range from INR 9,300 to INR 35,000 per month, while Group D stenographer salaries range from INR 5,000 to INR 20,000 per month. As of this writing, there are approximately 1,500 open positions. As compared to Grade C vacancies, there are significantly more or even twice as many Grade D positions available. A typing/skills test is also part of the application process.

Over 5,000 to 6,000 vacancies are filled each year by the Delhi police recruitment cell of the SSC, which conducts the recruitment process. Job satisfaction, social status, generous allowances, and additional perks such as the and Central Government Employees Group Insurance Scheme (CGEGIS) and National Pension Scheme (NPS) all contribute to the job's appeal. The salary ranges from INR 21,000 to INR 69,000, but these factors aren't the only ones that make the job exciting.

In addition to serving as police officers, constables can also rise to become ACPs and DYSPs (Deputy Superintendent of Police). You may be turned off by the selection process's physical fitness requirements. Prior to submitting an application, please keep that in mind.

The LIC AAO & AE Job Openings
The Life Insurance Corporation of India (LIC), India's largest insurance company, uses this process to hire AAOs and AEs on a regular basis to maintain its monopoly in the insurance industry. Legal, Chartered Accountant, Actuarial, Information Technology (IT), and Rajbhasha positions are filled by Asst. Administrative Officers.

Depending on what kind of job you're aiming for, the requirements will be different. About INR 62,000 is earned by LIC AAOs and INR 57,000 by LIC Assistant Engineers. Candidates for the LIC AE recruitment must have a bachelor's degree in engineering. One of the most important requirements for the LIC AAO recruitment process is the possession of an undergraduate or graduate degree from an accredited institution. However, there are only 50-200 openings available, compared to SSC, UPSC, etcmuch .'s larger pool of applicants. If you want to climb the corporate ladder quickly and reap corresponding financial rewards, this is a must-apply exam.

BSNL Recruitment for Junior Engineers
If you were hoping to land a high-paying engineering position at a well-known company, now is the time to do so. Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited, better known as BSNL, has over 2 000 openings for junior engineers even in the midst of a pandemic. Engineering students, as the name implies, are the only ones who can take advantage of this opportunity. BSNL's Jr. Engineers can earn up to INR 40,000 per year. For aspiring engineers, job stability is a major selling point. Over 60 percent of India's 9 lakh engineers graduate each year without a job, and this is a breath of fresh air for the industry.

There are a plethora of other possibilities as well. You could apply for a slew of other positions as well. Exams for the State Public Service Commission (State PSC), banks, and the UPSC should all be considered, and the search should not end there.

Getting a job may seem impossible in these difficult times, and looking for your first one can be exhausting, but don't give up. Keep an eye out on job boards like Naukri, AngelList, and LinkedIn for openings that match your skills and experience, and work on your resume and profile to make yourself more marketable.
