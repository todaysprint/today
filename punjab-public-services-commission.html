What is a Foundation Course in UPSC?

India's Civil Service Examination (CSE) ranks among the country's most prestigious exams. To fill vacancies in the country's 24 civil service branches, the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) holds an annual nationwide exam.

More than 17 million candidates took the UPSC preliminary examinations in 2018. Fewer than a thousand aspirants were selected to become Indian civil servants in the end. A candidate must prepare for at least a year to pass the three rounds of examinations.

The best training for this goal necessitates students to relocate to other cities. However, there is now the option of taking a UPSC online course, which is more convenient and cost-effective.

To begin with, what is "Foundational"?

Foundation courses for the UPSC are designed to lay the groundwork for success in the exam, as the name implies. From one school to the next, the length of time it takes to complete a foundation course is different.

Between 10 months and three years is a reasonable range to aim for. Aside from these other factors, the cost of a course will also vary depending on the quality of the instructor and the course's success rate

Those who are new to the field can take a foundation course.
It is possible to take a UPSC foundation course before, during, and after college. Preparation for the exam can be expedited by beginning as early as possible. You can enrol in the foundation course while still in high school or after you've completed your high school diploma.

It is essential to determine if you have the time to take a foundation course while pursuing your education. If time is an issue, a UPSC online course can make things more convenient.

Why Consider Taking a Foundation Course?
If you're considering taking a foundation course, you may wonder whether or not it's necessary. The relevance of a foundation course in passing the UPSC exam is undeniable, even if you have a strong self-study habit.

A foundation course has the following advantages.

In order to succeed in the civil service exam, aspirants, especially young students, need to develop the right mindset.
Preliminary and main exams and interview rounds can be better prepared for through experienced guidance. Additionally, the study aids are a big help.
A solid foundation is laid, and this allows students to better understand and respond to test questions that require in-depth knowledge. The NCERT syllabus is emphasised in UPSC foundation courses because most questions are derived from it.
Based on individual performances, it provides expert assessment and subsequent guidance.
How Do You Pick a College?
As a prospective UPSC foundation student, you are likely to feel apprehensive. Foundation courses can be found at a wide range of institutions, from small to large. To avoid wasting time and money, you must conduct thorough research before enrolling in the programme.

Accessibility
A physical institution or an online course must be chosen. Check whether or not the course materials are easily accessible, regardless of where you are. A repository of course materials in various formats, such as video, image, and audio, will be available to students taking online courses. The easier it is to use, the better it is.

Authenticity
Prior to enrolling in any institution of higher learning, make sure it has a high degree of credibility. To entice prospective students, many institutions will make up or exaggerate data about their past performance. You should try to find out what their success rate is if you can. In addition, former students can be a good source of information about their own experiences.

Syllabus and Instructors
On the websites of many legitimate online courses, students can find information about the instructors. Pay close attention to the credentials of the individuals you're considering hiring. Also, make certain that the course covers the primary and preliminary syllabi in full.
