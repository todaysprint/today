SSC CGL Recruitment: What Are Top 17 Job Posts And Why?

We all desire a job that is well-respected in society because it makes us feel accomplished. In order to achieve their goal of working for the government, many students have always been eager to apply for the SSC CGL process. I hope you meet the SSC CGL Cut Off to begin your career in the field of your choice.

There's a good chance that you've already applied or are planning to apply in the future. Candidates are asked to select a position of interest when they apply. SSC CGL Exam Dates are approaching, and you may be wondering about the various job options, features, responsibilities, and compensation scales that are available. Put an end to your conundrum because we're going to go into great detail about it.

Pay and Responsibilities for the Top 17 Positions:

There are 33 job openings being filled through the SSC CGL Recruitment process at the moment. Candidates will have a plethora of options from which to choose, which is both exciting and perplexing at the same time. As a result, we've compiled a list of 17 popular positions, each with a range of compensation and responsibilities. The 9300-34800 salary range is the only thing that connects all of these job listings. Check it out – I'd like to see it for myself.

The position of Income Tax Inspector has always been in high demand because it involves a lot of travel and raiding. They also conduct assessments, verifications, and processing of IT records for individuals and businesses.
Working as an assistant in the Ministry of External Affairs entails a wide range of responsibilities, such as sending and receiving emails and phone calls and typing reports and keeping files up to date.
Please tell me if you're interested in becoming an Assistant Audit Officer. This position is tasked with performing audits of government and non-profit organisations. Choosing auditing if you've always been interested is a good decision.
Inspector/Examiner positions require you to perform clerical and file-related tasks.
There are a variety of responsibilities associated with the Inspector/Central Excise position, such as clerical/filing work, the detection of tax evasion, and more.
As an assistant in the AFHQ, candidates would be expected to perform a variety of administrative tasks, such as maintaining reports and following up on cases on a regular basis.
Assistant in the Ministry of Railway is a good option if you want to work in the rail industry. Clerk work is part of this position.
Anyone who is an assistant in the Intelligence Bureau should be proud of themselves. You will be hired as a computer and data-related investigator.
To become an Inspector/Preventive Officer, you must have a bachelor's degree in a related field, as well as a working knowledge of Microsoft Word and Excel.
Money laundering cases are under the watchful eye of the Assistant Enforcement Officer.
Since candidates would be performing a variety of tasks, including updating case files, maintaining records, keeping an eye on vigilance activities and contacting respective depths, the Assistant (Central Vigilance Commission) is considered to be a bit different among others.
Assistant Section Officer (CSS) is the next position, and you'll be responsible for preparing various notes, studies, and reports for your superiors to review and approve.
With the help of state police, CBI sub-inspectors will be able to investigate crimes. Candidates will be required to complete 32 weeks of training. It's not an easy job, to be sure.
Government agencies such as the Department of Personnel & Training, Parliamentary Affairs, and the Election Commission all employ Assistant (Other Ministries).
In order to carry out auditing duties, a divisional accountant (CAG) has been hired. In this position, you'll be responsible for a smaller number of transfers. It's worth it if you're interested in CAG.
There will be an Inspector (Narcotics) who will be in charge of keeping an eye out for illegal drug trafficking. They also perform regular platform checks.
The Department of Personnel and Training Parliamentary Affairs is one of several government agencies that the Assistant (Other Ministries) is expected to assist.
