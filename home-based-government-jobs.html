Essential Details About The UPSC NDA Exam

When it comes to joining the Indian army, courage and prestige are required. It's an incredible feeling to know that you're responsible for the safety and well-being of the entire country. Being a member of the military requires one to give more than just their time and effort; they must also sacrifice their life. The life of an army, navy, or air force member is unquestionably difficult, but the sense of fulfilment that comes with it is incomparable. The most sought-after path to joining the armed forces is the national level UPSC NDA examination. If you share these passions for the uniform, country, and patriotism, then this article is for you.


The National Defense Academy (NDA) and the Indian Naval Academy (INA) conduct national level examinations to select qualified candidates (INAC). UPSC NDA is the name of the test, which is administered twice a year. We'll learn everything we need to know about the UPSC NDA exam in this article. If you want to serve your country by joining the armed forces, make sure you read this article and study for the UPSC NDA exam in 2022.


What is the UPSC NDA Exam?
We've outlined the UPSC NDA examination process in the sections that follow. As a result, carefully review the information provided below.

Getting into the military via the UPSC National Defence Academy exam is one of the most sought-after methods.
Applicants for admission to the National Defense Academy (NDA) and Indian Naval Academy (INA) are selected by the commission through a national-level examination.
The UPSC selects students for the National Defense Academy on a biannual basis.
In addition, review the complete UPSC NDA syllabus and begin preparing for the UPSC NDA written exam as soon as possible.

What Is The UPSC NDA Exam Eligibility Criteria?
A thorough understanding of the commission's national-level examination's eligibility requirements is essential.

To begin, a candidate must meet certain age requirements in order to sit for the UPSC NDA exam.
Candidates must have at least a 12th-grade education. A 12th-grade diploma from any subject is required for entry into the army wing. The navy and the air force, on the other hand, require a high school diploma with a strong background in physics and mathematics.
The UPSC NDA examination was previously only open to male candidates. Female candidates can now sit for the exam and join the Indian Naval Academy or the National Defense Academy, thanks to a Supreme Court ruling.
How Does The UPSC NDA Select Candidates?
We can learn a lot about the UPSC NDA's selection process from the information provided below.

The UPSC NDA examination has two stages of selection: the written examination and the services selection board (SSB) interview.
In the written exam, there are two sections: one for general ability questions and one for math questions.
Candidate selection is based on a stringent interview process that includes an evaluation of one's intelligence and psychological make-up.
Finally, based on the number of openings, their ranking on the merit list, their medical fitness, and their personal preferences, qualified applicants are assigned to the military services of the land, sea, or air.
