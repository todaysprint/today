The selection process for SBI POs
Each round is equally important, and thus qualifying for the next round is necessary until the final selection process is completed.

SBI PO Preliminary.
The SBI PO Prelims Exam measures a candidate's general aptitude in the initial stages of the recruitment process.
It is a one-hour online objective test for the SBI PO Preliminary Exam.
There are three sections: English Language, Reasoning Ability and Quantitative Aptitude.
There will be no sectional cut-offs for the SBI PO Prelims Exams.
The bank, on the other hand, will offset a general reduction based on the categories of applicants.
Ten times the number of vacancies (approximately) in each category will be shortlisted for the Main Examination based on the merits listed above.


In the second phase, SBI PO Mains.
In order to sit for the SBI PO Main Exam, candidates must pass the preliminary exam.
Each of the SBI PO Mains Exam sections has an objective and descriptive component.
Logical Reasoning & Computers, English Language, Quantitative Aptitude, and Economy/General/Banking Awareness are all part of the SBI PO Mains Exam.
The 50-point descriptive test follows immediately after the objective test.
Each category's final score in the Main Exam will be used to compile a final merit list.
Sections will not be cut off.
It is expected that three times the number of openings in each category will be filled by candidates who have met or exceeded the minimum aggregate qualifying score. Those candidates will be shortlisted for the Group Exercise and Interview.
the SBI PO group interview and exercises (stage three)
After a lengthy selection process, the process has now come to an end. SBI Mains Exam winners will be invited to participate in a group exercise and an interview. As a candidate's fate is decided in this round, this is the most important.

In order to become an SBI PO, a candidate must perform well in the SBI Mains and Interviews.
