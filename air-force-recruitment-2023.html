How To Prepare Yourself For UPSC IAS Prelims?

You should put your career first and foremost. The exam is approaching, and instead of worrying about how you'll do, focus on how you'll prepare for it. Candidates, on the other hand, are likely to feel low or self-doubt. It should not be used to your advantage. Having a clear plan of action is essential. To get the best results, you must put all of your attention on yourself.

The syllabus for the upsc IAS preliminaries has you stumped. We're going to make things a little easier for you right now, so stay with us. Let's take a look at what you can do to get yourself ready –

First Things First – Get to Work!


Preparation for the UPSC prelims should begin at least a year in advance. You should select a subject that appears on both the mains syllabus and the preliminary syllabus. Preparation for the exam is a solitary endeavour. Preparation should begin at least three months prior to the preliminary examinations.

2. Improve Your English – You won't have any problems if your English is adequate. For those who are lacking in this area, you'll need to devise a unique practise schedule. The UPSC IAS GS 2023 Coaching in English is a great option for those who want to prepare for the exam on their own. Over there, you'll be surrounded by the ideal conditions. Your exam-related anxiety will dissipate.

Make Sure Your Plan Is Correct – Make sure your plan is correct before going ahead with it. You'd have to cook for yourself at home. All of your effort should be put into an ideal study plan. A variety of distractions and temptations may be preventing you from concentrating on your preparations. You must be honest with yourself. You must keep in mind that if you don't put in the effort, you won't get anything.


Prepare for the CSAT exam by studying the syllabus, which helps you assess your ability to reason logically and critically. Candidates with a strong background in mathematics, science, or engineering will breeze through the exam with little to no preparation time required. Practicing mock exams or previous years' UPSC CSAT question papers is all that is required. If you want the best results, put them into practise.

Most candidates believe that they should focus solely on figures and facts. However, this is a mistake. In fact, this is completely false. Additionally, the UPSC must verify whether or not you are aware of the information. They test your ability to analyse the subject matter in an ideal way. A timed test of your speed and number of questions answered in a given period is also part of the process.
