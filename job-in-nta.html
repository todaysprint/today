How to Improve Your Calculation Speed in Any Competitive Exam

Any competitive exam has a tough section called quantitative aptitude. Many students are afraid of this section, but if they plan ahead and practise hard, it has the potential to be the most difficult section on the exam.

The candidate, on the other hand, must have a solid grasp of the concepts, as well as a few core skills, one of which is speed.

In any competitive exam, a candidate with a high level of speed is more likely to perform well in the quantitative aptitude section.

A few tips and tricks on how to improve your calculation speed so as to maximise the number of attempts and thus the score on any competitive exam will be discussed here.

Practice
The more different kinds of sums a student does, the more likely he or she is to be able to solve complex problems, analyse them, and answer reasoning questions quickly and accurately. This is critical, especially in subjects like statistics, measurement, and quadratic equations, which often require extensive computations.

Methods of approximation
Long, difficult problems on quantitative ability are common in many competitive exams. Solving them requires a great deal of time and effort. As a result, no one will be able to stop students from improving their grades and passing their exams if they develop approximation strategies for faster calculations, increasing accuracy by nearly tenfold. Sets, Indices, and more can all benefit from this.

Conceptual Clarity
The first step is to make sure that the fundamentals and concepts are understood thoroughly. In arithmetic, this can speed up the process of getting a solution. Before learning a formula or short trick, make sure you understand the underlying principles so that if you ever forget it, you can quickly and easily derive it during the test.

Formulas and Quick Tips are essential to mastering the game.
Formulas and short tricks can be used whenever possible to solve arithmetical problems' sums. Thus, mastering various formulas and short tricks will aid in quickly solving long, complex questions.
