Five Pocket-Friendly Apps for Competitive Exam Preparations

Five Apps for Competitive Exam Preparation That Fit in Your Pocket
In the last few years, there have been a number of significant technological breakthroughs. A major technological breakthrough, such as the smartphone, has had and continues to have a significant impact on our daily lives.

Basic phone features like making and receiving phone calls and sending text messages are now enhanced by smartphone features like web browsing, social networking, reading news and other entertainment media, and even online shopping. Smartphones have revolutionised the way we live our daily lives.

 

More and more of our waking hours are now spent on our smartphones, thanks to the convenience and power they provide. And we've found it to be one of the most user-friendly devices.

E-learning Apps have been developed for students to use on their smartphones to help them prepare for various competitive exams more cost-effectively and more easily.

 

Here are a few of the most popular and cost-effective smartphone apps for students preparing for competitive exams.

Testbook
Aspirants preparing for government competitive exams frequently use the Testbook exam preparation app. The app serves as a one-stop shop for all of a student's educational and guidance needs.

As a result, it provides access to 200+ competitive exams including all major government examinations like SSC and banking examinations, RRB and the military's GATE exam as well as UPSC and DRDO's UGC NET and CTET free practise tests.

Aspirants who are preparing for government exams use Testbook because it has one of the largest collections of free online practise tests for students to use.

 

Also included are over a hundred online courses covering more than 200 exams. There are also PFDs and Study notes, classes for exam strategy and mock tests with detailed solutions and in-depth performance analysis included in these courses.

It also provides free daily, weekly and monthly current affairs content for a variety of exam preparations. Updates are made to this along with the course and test series. Last but not least, it gives students the ability to download videos and save them for offline viewing, to make notes and screenshots, to save important questions and tests, and to ask questions in real-time to knowledgeable professors.

Gradeup
For the past two years, Gradeup, owned by Times Internet, has helped millions of students across India prepare for various government exams. All the major competitive exams like banking exams, RRB exams, UPSC examinations, SSC exams, teaching exams, GATE examinations, JEE examinations, and more can be found on the company's website and app under the guidance of expert teachers.

 

It was the goal of the grade up app's inception to cultivate a supportive network of mentors and students. Each of these groups has been formed around a specific exam. There are many ways in which students help and encourage each other in the classroom, such as solving each other's doubts or questions and taking regular quizzes and mock exams.

The Google Playstore has the Gradeup app for free. Using the grade up app, students can access free mock tests, quizzes, study notes, and solved papers to help them prepare for various competitive exams. They can also pay a small fee each month or year to access all of the app's content.

Accuracy in a Pocket
Pocket aptitude is a free Android app that helps students prepare for exams. The app was created for those preparing for competitive exams such as UPSC, CAT, banking exams, RRB, SSC, GATE, CMATE, and more that require strong quantitative aptitude and logical reasoning skills.

To help students improve their quantitative and logical reasoning skills, the app's content is carefully selected. It includes more than 1800 questions, practise tests, study material, formulas, detailed solutions, and more. The app includes questions of various difficulty levels, as well as a feature that allows students to bookmark questions and tests so that they can review and retake them at a later time.

Add 247 to your collection.
Live video classes, free unlimited mock tests, 20000+ quizzes, study notes, free previous year question papers, and exam strategy videos are just some of the features of the Adda 247 app. Aside from this feature, it provides a one-stop shop for all students' general knowledge needs, with regularly updated current events and GK.

In addition to Hindi and English, the app's content is accessible in both languages. On top of that, you can save questions and content for offline access via the app's user interface, which is simple to navigate. Detailed answers are provided for every question in the test series, quizzes, and practise sets.

Unacademy
This popular online learning platform in India provides a wide range of educational resources, including live classes taught by well-known educators and mentors as well as practise tests. Students can use this to get answers to their questions and test their knowledge in real time. In addition to the free content, students can subscribe to Unacademy to gain full access to all of the educational resources and content available.

Unacademy has helped more than 30,000 students achieve their educational and career goals thanks to its specialised courses for a wide range of competitive exams, including all major government exams.

Numerous students have written in to tell us how Unacademy has aided them in achieving success in some of India's most difficult competitive exams. Unacdemy is a popular choice for exam aspirants because it has teamed up with experts in the field to design courses based on the latest exam pattern and syllabus.
