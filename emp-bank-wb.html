5 Must Attempt Government Exams

Everyone in our country, from the postman to the Prime Minister, is a government employee. Regardless of your field of expertise or skill set, the government sector provides a wide range of options.

Even in the event of a pandemic, a government position is financially stable and secure. There are numerous advantages to working for the government, such as paid time off, allowances, health insurance, and a respectable retirement income.
Recruiting organisations administer exams all year long to identify qualified applicants for a variety of positions within government agencies. Here are the greatest Government tests that can help you land a high-paying career in the public sector. '


The SSC CHSL is an acronym for the SSC
For the State Selection Commission Higher Secondary Level Exam, SSC CHSL is the abbreviation. Due to the fact that only those who have completed their high school education are eligible to apply for these positions, the SSC CHSL exam is easier than other SSC exams.

Data entry operators (DEOs), postal assistants (PAs), and junior secretariat assistants (JSAs) are among the positions for which the SSC CHSL is held. For the SSC CHSL, the compensation ranges from $19,900 to $19,100, depending on the position you hold.

Despite the fact that the SSC CHSL Exam Pattern and syllabus are the same as those for the SSC CGL, the SSC CHSL is a lot easier than the SSC CGL. If you want to secure a solid government job before the end of the year, the SSC CHSL may be your best bet.
With the correct SSC CHSL Books, your preparation can go from dreadful to astronomically better.

The SSC CGL is an acronym
The Staff Selection Commission Combined Graduate Level test is known as the SSC CGL by its abbreviation. In order to fill vacancies in various Indian agencies, ministries, and organisations, the SSC holds the SSC CGL exam each year.
In order to hire new employees for Group B and Group C positions, the SSC CGL exam is administered. People from a variety of backgrounds can apply for positions in Group B and C, which are non-technical in nature. Income tax inspectors, customs inspectors and preventative officers, as well as tax assistants with the CBDT and CBEC, are among the positions available through the SSC CGL.

A graduate who is between the ages of 20 and 32 is eligible to apply for the SSC CGL examination. SSC CGL exams are usually held in October-December every year, with formal notice published in May-June.

A candidate's salary depends on the position they choose. With additional benefits, the salary ranges from Rs.18,000 to Rs.2,50,000 per year.

The GATE Exam
Master's degree candidates and employees for PSUs (Public Sector Companies like BHEL, ONGC, IOCL, etc.) are selected through GATE, which is held worldwide.

GATE is a joint effort by Indian Institutes of Science and Indian Institutes of Technology (Roorkee, Delhi, Guwahati, Chennai, Kanpur, Kharagpur, and Mumbai). Graduate Aptitude Test for Engineering (GATE) is open to students in the final year of their undergraduate degree in engineering.

Candidates who have qualified for national level competitive exams, such as UPSC/AICTE, may also take this test. Every year, GATE takes place in the month of February.

As the Union Public Service Commission Central Civil Service Examination (UPSCCSE), the UPSC conducts the Civil Services Exam to select competent candidates for various positions in the Indian civil services.

Indian Administrative Service (IAS), Indian Police Service (IPS), and Indian Foreign Services (IFS) are among the 24 services that recruit applicants (IFS). As a general rule, the preliminary rounds take place in June of each year.

National Eligibility and Eligibility Test for Undergraduates (UG
NTA, on behalf of UGC, administers the National Eligibility Test (UGC NET) to find applicants for the Assistant Professor position, which includes the possibility of receiving a Junior Research Fellowship, at Indian universities and colleges.

To sit for this exam, you must have a master's degree. Students in their last year of a master's degree are also eligible to take the exam. As a result, once graduation, they will be able to get a well-paying position with a reputable employer.

Every year, the UGC NET is held in June. Candidates who pass the exam will receive a remuneration that is determined by the universities to which they are assigned. Salaries at private universities often range from Rs. 35,000 to Rs. 60,000, while those at public institutions typically range from Rs. 70,000 to Rs. 1,20,000.
