11 Innovative Ways to Use Technology in Education

According to recent studies, technology has had a significant impact on student achievement. Many educational institutions are embracing technology, despite the fact that classroom teaching is still prevalent. Textbooks are being replaced by digital whiteboards, flip classrooms, and Chromebooks. It's in the teachers' best interest to back this cause.

Take Your Students on a Virtual Tour of the World
Involvement in the community is a great way to learn about it. All of us do it. Because of advances in educational technology, it is no longer possible to postpone or cancel field trips due to logistical issues. Google Cardboard can also be used to simulate a field trip.

Visit historic sites, learn about earth's natural phenomena, and travel to new places. Make an effort to captivate and enchant your students.

Class Schedules Can Be Maintained Online
Class schedules can be created and shared using Google Calendar. It informs students about the length of the class and the dates of upcoming events. Students would benefit greatly from receiving a link to your calendar via email. You'll be able to stay on top of things and help your students prepare for each class.

Utilize Computer-Assisted Intervention (CAI)
Mathematical concepts can be difficult to comprehend in their entirety. Traditional manipulatives, such as base ten blocks and coins, are less effective than virtual manipulatives. In order to be more widely adopted, these are new technologies.

Students can use virtual manipulatives to help them better understand difficult mathematical concepts. With virtual manipulatives, hands-on learners can be accommodated in the classroom with ease and appeal.

How do you create instructional videos?
More than reading, video aids in the retention of key concepts. Digital whiteboard explanations, classroom activities, and peer presentations can all be recorded in video format with Hippo Video's help. If you prefer, you can share them directly with your students through Drive or Google Classroom, as well as YouTube.

In the classroom, students are able to learn at their own pace, which appeals to visual learners. Using videos, you can build a stronger bond with your students and make complex concepts simpler for them to grasp.

Social Networks
Adults aren't the only ones using social media. Education can benefit from the integration of social media. There are many hashtags that you can create for students who have any questions or doubts regarding the course or homework they are assigned.

Answer students' questions by projecting the hashtag feed results onto a screen. Those who are hesitant to ask questions in front of a large group can benefit from this.

Surveys, quizzes, and video feedback
You could use video feedback to provide students with one-on-one feedback on their work. Peer feedback can also be given through video. Students would be able to speak their thoughts instead of writing them down.

A tool like Google Forms can be used to encourage students to create forms, quizzes, and polls that are relevant to their academic endeavours.

Students can use electronic devices in the classroom to create these. Teaching with this method could be very successful in the classroom.

Gamification
The best way to understand difficult concepts is to practise them first. All students are affected by this. Use it to teach complex concepts in a fun and engaging way!

Students can improve their typing speed by playing games in the computer lab. Using them to speed type is a blast!

Students may be asked to work in groups or pairs by their teachers, and they may be encouraged to play cooperatively while doing so. Students learn to collaborate with others, which is an important skill for their future professional lives.
