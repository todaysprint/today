The Best E-Learning Platforms That Students Can Benefit From

Student habits have shifted dramatically in the last few years in terms of where, when, how, and with whom they consume and share educational content. The ongoing digital revolution has fueled this seismic shift in the transfer of information. Unlike traditional classrooms, online lectures can be accessed whenever the student wants and as many times as they like.

Platforms for online coaching have revolutionised the educational industry in unprecedented ways. The cost-effectiveness of different courses increases when learning occurs quickly and easily. In addition, platforms can curate planned content for their target audience and take a more streamlined approach to carry out their mission.

Online education has many advantages, so let's take a look at some of the best e-learning platforms students can use to gain access to desired knowledge.

Salman Khan, an MIT and Harvard alumnus, founded Khan Academy in 2008 and has since grown into a global education powerhouse. One of Khan Academy's primary goals is the development of educational tools that can be used by students around the world. Content curated by experts in the field is available on the portal for every course and level you can think of. In addition to English, more than 20 other languages can be found on the website and the app's study materials.
In terms of online learning, it's safe to say that Khan Academy is the gold standard, with a mission to provide free, world-class education for anyone, anywhere. Taking the courses on their own or in conjunction with classroom instruction is a viable option for those looking to brush up on their skills. Khan Academy Kids, a new programme for children aged two to six, aims to prepare them for elementary school by teaching them the fundamentals of math and language.

IIT graduates launched Testbook in 2014, and it has grown rapidly since then. Recently, the EdTech startup Testbook has emerged as the fastest rising. More than 11 million students are currently part of its database of exams, courses, study guides, and more.
Testbook's YouTube channel has more than 100k subscribers, and their app has more than 6 million instals, all while helping students pass a variety of exams, including those in the government sector. It's also popular with students because of their live coaching sessions, online courses, and mock tests.

No doubt Testbook is reshaping test-taking strategies for the better, laying the groundwork for a more positive educational competitive environment.

in 2015, with the help of entrepreneur Ronnie Screwvala, three IIT-Delhi alums founded upGrad with the goal of laying the groundwork for tomorrow's workforce. It was decided that in a competitive industry, professionals should be able to easily upskill themselves on a regular basis to stay relevant. The best universities and industry experts joined forces with Upgrad to make this possible.
Some of India's largest online programmes in the areas of data technology and management have helped thousands of professionals achieve their career goals without any hassle since they began with Entrepreneurship programmes.

With upGrad's online education platform, students can access industry-relevant courses at any time, from any location, on any device.

Andrew Ng and Daphne Koller, both Stanford computer science professors, founded Coursera in 2012, and since then it has grown to be one of the largest online education providers in the world. There are a variety of degrees available through Coursera's collaborations with some of the world's most prestigious universities in a wide range of academic fields. It's possible to learn new skills, advance in your current career, and much more with this platform.
There are four to ten weeks of classes on their platform, with one hour of lectures per day, and the courses are usually accompanied by tests, weekly exercises, and assignments that are graded by other students. As of 2017, they have also begun offering full Master's degrees in a variety of academic fields.

It was founded in May 2012 by the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and Harvard University as one of the leading Massive Open Online Courses (MOOC) providers. Over 2500 online courses from 140 institutions can be accessed by pressing a single button. In addition to the previously mentioned schools, others with a well-known reputation are lending their support, including MIT, Harvard, UC Berkeley, and Boston University.
It is also used for research-oriented learnings of demographic behaviour, which are then used to improve the retention, course completion and learning outcomes of online and traditional on-campus courses. edX.
