All You Need to Know About SSC CGL Recruitment

The SSC conducts the SSC CGL exam for the selection of qualified candidates for various government departments. Services provided by subordinates, such as

Central Excise & Customs (Central) Assistant in various Ministries and Departments, Government of India Preventive Officers in Customs Examiner in Customs Sub-Inspector in CBI & Central Bureau of Narcotics Assistant Enforcement Officer, Department of Revenue Auditor, Divisional. Accountant & Junior. Accountant Tax Assistant in CBEC & CBDT
Those who pass the exam will be considered for jobs in Groups B and C. An online application is the first step in the SSC CGL Recruitment process. SSC has provided a link on its official website where interested individuals can submit their applications. Candidates who have graduated from college are eligible to apply.

Please fill out the SSC CGL online application.
Here are the steps you need to take to apply for the SSC CGL exam online:

Go to the SSC CGL website and sign up for an account.
Don't make any mistakes when you're filling out the application.
Credit Card/Debit Card/Wallets/Internet Banking/Cash Cards/etc. can be used to pay the exam application fee.
Fill out the application form with an email address that is active and valid. Keep it active until the results are announced because that's where you'll get all the notifications and updates.
Make sure you have a recent scanned photo of yourself and your signature on hand in accordance with the official notification's requirements.
Verify that the application form is error-free before submitting it.
The completed application form can be downloaded here. You can also save a copy for future reference by printing out this page.
Recruitment for the SSC CGL
Stages one through four of the SSC CGL Recruitment process are:

It will be a computer-based online test in Stage I of the SSC CGL Tier 1. At most, there will be 100 questions on the test. The test will have a maximum score of 200 and a time limit of 60 minutes. Every incorrect answer on the exam is penalised with a 0.50 penalty.
This stage is only open to those candidates who have already passed the tier-I exam. There will also be an online test for Tier II of the SSC CGL exam. There will be a total of two papers in this collection, with an additional four papers designated for specific positions. There are two papers, one for Quantitative Aptitude and one for English Language & Comprehension. A total of 200 questions will be asked in the Paper II. Paper-I will have a maximum score of 200, and Paper-II will have a maximum score of 200. There will be a negative marking of 0.50 points for each incorrect answer in Tier-II as well.
Stage III: A 100-mark descriptive paper will be given in SSC CGL Tier III. It won't be accessible. Step four is an SSC CGL. To qualify for Tier IV of the SSC CGL Recruitment process, candidates must pass the Data Entry Skill Test (DEST) or the Computer Proficiency Test (CPT). The DEST will only be held for the position of Tax Assistant.
The syllabus for the SSC CGL exam
The Tier-I syllabus for SSC CGL is as follows:
It includes everything from sports to history to economics to geography to general politicking to biology to chemistry.
quantitative aptitude: percentages (proportions), ratios (proportions), profits (profit and loss), time and work (average), simple and compound interests (time and distance), mixtures and alligations (mixtures and combinations), probability, permutations and combinations, trigonometry (data interpretation), geometry (measuring) and trigonometry (interpretation of data).
Intelligence and Reasoning in General
Comprehension of the English Language
The following is the Tier-II syllabus for the SSC CGL examination:
Aspirants' arithmetic abilities will be tested in Paper 1. However, the difficulty level of the questions will be significantly higher than in Tier-I. You'll learn about a wide range of topics such as whole numbers and decimals and fractions, as well as percentiles and averages, discount rates, arithmetic operations, ratios, time and distance, and tables and graphs.
Paper 2: This paper is designed to measure your proficiency in the English language. The paper will be written at a 10+2 level of quality. Syllabus topics include Grammar, Vocabulary, Synonyms & Antonyms, Sentence Completion and Spellings, Phrases and Idioms, Sentence Structure and Comprehension, etc. in Paper 2.
The Tier-III syllabus for the SSC CGL is as follows: It's going to be an in-depth essay. Aspirants' writing abilities will be put to the test. Letter and application writing will be part of the assignment. Letters to the editor, letters to the municipality, and other forms of formal and informal correspondence will all be part of the letter-writing process (letter to a friend, letter to relatives, etc.).
